Blood Pressure + Cholesterol

Blood Pressure vs. Cholesterol

Cholesterol causes plaque build up in the arteries. When this happens, the heart has to pump twice as hard to provide oxygen and blood to your body which leads to high blood pressure.

Fasting Blood Pressure in Relation to Heart Disease

Fasting bllod sugar levels give vital clues about how a person's body is managing blood sugar. Levels greater than 120 mg/dl is an indicator that the patient can be at risk of coronary heart disease. In our dataset, there was a higher percentage of people who had fasting blood sugar levels less than 120 mg/dl.

Heart Disease Frequency According to FBS

Although there is a correlation between Fasting Blood Sugar and Heart Disease not all people who suffer from heart disease have FBS higher than 120 mg/dl. In our dataset, there was a lower percentage of people who have and don't have heart disease that also had fasting blood sugar greater than 120 mg/dl.

Thalassemia & Heart Disease

Thalassemia is a genetic blood disorder that affects the red blood cells. When the red blood cells are abnormal or you don't have enough, this affects the bodies natural ability to carry oxygen throughout the body. Based on the graph, you can see that Level 2 and 3 are the worst levels because it increases your chances of heart disease.

Relation Between Resting ECG & Target

Checking Resting ECG's is important because doctors check your heart rhythm for any irregularities and compare them to the exercised induced heart rate. 0 is considered normal heart rhythms, 1, is abnormal heart rhythms, and 2 is severe heart rhythms.
